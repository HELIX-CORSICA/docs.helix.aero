# Quick Start

Follow these steps to install Helix Flight Planner and produce your first aerial survey plan.

## Step 1: Download the App

Visit [app.helix.aero/download](https://app.helix.aero/download) and pick your platform.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Windows">
    Download the **Helix-FlightPlanner-Setup-x64.exe** installer for Windows 10 or later (64-bit).
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="macOS">
    Download the **Helix-FlightPlanner-macOS.dmg** disk image for macOS 12 Monterey or later.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Step 2: Install and Launch

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Windows">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Run the installer">
        Launch the downloaded `.exe` and follow the wizard. The default install path is `C:\Program Files\Helix Flight Planner`.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Start the app">
        Open **Helix Flight Planner** from the Start menu or the desktop shortcut. The app opens directly on the login screen.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="macOS">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Mount the DMG">
        Open the downloaded `.dmg` and drag **Helix Flight Planner** into your Applications folder.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Launch from Applications">
        Open the app from Applications or Spotlight. On first launch, macOS may show a Gatekeeper prompt — the build is notarized, so click **Open** to proceed.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Step 3: Log In and Activate

On the login screen, enter your Helix Aero credentials. If you do not have an account, visit [app.helix.aero](https://app.helix.aero) to register or request a trial.

Activation happens automatically on first login — the app sends your machine fingerprint to the licensing server and registers a seat.

<Note>
  Your license allows offline use for up to 7 days. After that, the app re-verifies online before you can keep editing missions.
</Note>

If the **Slot limit exceeded** message appears, manage your registered machines at [app.helix.aero](https://app.helix.aero).

## Step 4: First Launch Onboarding

On first launch, Helix Flight Planner shows a short tooltip overlay that introduces the main workspace: the dashboard, the mission wizard, the 3D globe, the planning panel and the export menu. You can replay it any time from **Help > Onboarding**.

## Step 5: Create Your First Mission

From the dashboard, click **New Mission**. The mission wizard walks you through six short steps:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Project and name">
    Give the mission a name and optionally attach it to a project. Missions created without a project appear at the top of the dashboard as "orphan" missions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Location">
    Search a place by name or pin it on the map. This centers the globe when the mission opens.
  </Step>
  <Step title="CRS">
    Pick the coordinate reference system. The default is WGS84 with automatic UTM zone, and a manual EPSG code is also supported.
  </Step>
  <Step title="DEM source">
    Choose the terrain source: **SRTM 30 m** (default, worldwide), **Copernicus GLO-30** (higher quality in mountainous terrain) or **None** (flat terrain).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sensor">
    Pick a sensor from the catalog and, if relevant, its focal length.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Aircraft">
    Pick an aircraft type. This seeds default altitude, speed and overlap values for the mission.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Click **Create**. The globe opens on your location with the planning panel on the right.

## Step 6: Draw the Area of Interest

On the 3D globe, open the drawing toolbar (left side, under the layer panel):

- Click **Draw** to start a polygon AOI, then click to place each vertex. Close the polygon by clicking the first point or pressing **Enter**.
- Click **Draw corridor** to draw a linear corridor for strip-mode missions (roads, rivers, pipelines).
- Click **Recenter** to fly the camera back to the AOI.

As soon as the polygon is closed, flight strips are computed automatically and appear on the globe.

## Step 7: Review and Export

Use the **Parametres** tab of the planning panel on the right to adjust altitude, overlap, sidelap and heading. Open the **Bandes** tab to review or edit individual strips, and the **Resume** tab to see mission statistics and QC indicators.

When you are happy with the plan, click **Export** in the top toolbar and pick a format — KML, PDF, CSV, GPX, XLSX or the native `.helix` container you load locally into the Helix FMS.
